# Build the field first

Private companies may be the players on the field, but they cannot perform if the field itself is unfinished.

That is the core argument in the Korean post about the role of government and parliament in the future of KRW stablecoins.

### Two things the public sector should do

1. **Lay the road by expanding financial infrastructure.** Stablecoin issuers need safe, short-duration assets for reserve management. That means practical instruments such as short-dated government bonds and better access to key market plumbing.
2. **Fix the traffic lights by modernizing outdated rules.** If regulation bans realistic product behavior or creates unclear side paths, innovation moves elsewhere. The goal should be a predictable main road, not arbitrary obstacles.

### What good policy looks like

The role of the state is not to micromanage every private actor. It is to create a system where trustworthy actors can compete, customers can be protected, and the market structure supports long-term innovation instead of short-term improvisation.

### The bigger point

In this framing, public policy is not the opposite of innovation. It is the condition that makes durable innovation possible.
